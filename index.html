1. Song Architecture

Verse: The "Who, What, Where." Keep details specific here.

Chorus: The "Big Idea." Should be catchy and easy to remember.

Bridge: The "Twist." Change the melody or the emotional angle.

2. Rhyme Schemes (The Patterns)

  • AABB: Simple, nursery-rhyme feel.
  • ABAB: Classic pop/folk alternating feel.
  • XAXA: Only even lines rhyme. Very modern.

3. Advanced Rhyming Types

  • Perfect Rhyme: Cat / Hat.
  • Slant (Near) Rhyme: Storm / Alone. Sounds more natural/cool.
  • Internal Rhyme: Rhyming words inside the line.

4. The "Rule of Threes"

Repeat a phrase three times in a chorus to make it stick in the listener's brain.

5. Melodic Vowels

Use open vowels like AH, OH, and AY for long notes in your Chorus.

6. Sensory Writing

Don't say "I'm lonely." Describe "the echo in an empty hallway" or "the cold side of the bed."

7. Starting with the Chorus (Reverse Method)

The "Big Idea" First: Write your Chorus first to define the core emotion. Once you have the Chorus, ask yourself:

  • "How did I get here?" (This becomes Verse 1)
  • "What happened next?" (This becomes Verse 2)
  • "What is the one thing I haven't said yet?" (This becomes the Bridge)

Tip: If the Chorus is "High Energy," make the Verses "Low Energy" to create contrast.
