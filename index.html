Letter Theory Breakdown

Letters (A, B, C) are maps for rhymes. Same letters must rhyme together.

A: Your first rhyme sound.

B: A second, different rhyme sound.

ABAB: Line 1 & 3 rhyme. Line 2 & 4 rhyme. (Alternating/Cross Rhyme).

AABB: Line 1 & 2 rhyme. Line 3 & 4 rhyme. (Couplets).

Slant Rhymes: (e.g., "Park" and "Heart"). Sounds that are close but not perfect. Modern and cool.

Songwriting Bible

Close

1. Sensory Imagery (The VAK Rule)

Make the listener feel like they are there by using all three senses:

  • Visual: What is seen? (The neon light flickering).
  • Auditory: What is heard? (The gravel crunching under tires).
  • Kinesthetic: What is felt? (The cold sweat on your palms).

Pro Tip: Try to include one of each in every Verse.

2. Opening Line Hooks

Never start boring. Use one of these three strategies:

  • The Setting: Give a time and place. (3 AM at the diner).
  • The Action: Start in the middle of a fight or move. (I threw the keys).
  • The Statement: State a bold truth. (I never loved this town).

3. Chorus "Lift" Techniques

The Chorus needs to feel "bigger" than the Verse:

Vowel Extension: Use words with long vowels (Stay, Go, High, Home). These allow the singer to hold notes longer, creating a "hook."

Melodic Contrast: If your Verse is fast and wordy, make your Chorus slow and simple.

4. Stress, Meter & Cadence

The Breath Rule: A line is too long if a singer can't finish it comfortably in one breath.

Word Stress: Make sure the "High" notes of your music land on the most important words of the sentence (e.g., "I love YOU" instead of "I LOVE you").

5. The Narrative Arc (Storytelling)

Verse 1: Establish the "Stasis" (Life as it is now).

Verse 2: The "Conflict" (What changed or went wrong?).

Bridge: The "Realization" (The big 'Aha!' moment or emotional peak).

6. The 7 Universal Themes

Stuck? Pick a theme: Love/Loss, Rebellion, Aspiration, Nostalgia, Identity, Observation, or Escape.
