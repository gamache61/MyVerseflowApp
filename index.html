Letter Theory Cheat Sheet

A: The "Anchor." This sound tells the listener where the rhyme home is.

B: The "Balance." This sound provides the response or the secondary beat.

ABAB (Cross Rhyme): Creates a "walking" feel. Very melodic and popular in pop/rock.

AABB (Couplets): Creates a "punchy" feel. High impact, great for rapping or fast verses.

ABCB: The "Storyteller." Line 1 and 3 are unrhymed, making the lyrics feel more like natural speech.

The Songwriting Bible

Close

1. Common Song Structures

Structure organizes the listener's journey:

  • Verse-Chorus-Verse-Chorus-Bridge-Chorus: The Industry Standard.
  • AABA: Verse, Verse, Bridge, Verse. Common in older soul and jazz.
  • Verse-Refrain: No big chorus, just a repeating line at the end of each verse (e.g., Bob Dylan style).

2. Sensory Imagery (VAK)

Don't just tell us how you feel; show us the world. Use Visual (red tail lights), Auditory (static on the radio), and Kinesthetic (cold rain on skin).

Rule of thumb: Use at least two senses in every Verse.

3. The "Chorus Lift" & Power Vowels

The Chorus should feel like it "opens up." Use long vowel sounds like "Oh," "Ah," and "Ay" to allow the singer to hold notes. Keep the Chorus simple and universal so everyone can sing along.

4. Tension and Release

Songwriting is about building pressure and letting it go. Verses build the tension (the problem), and the Chorus provides the release (the emotion). The Bridge should be the "Aha!" moment where the perspective changes entirely.

5. Lyric Perspectives

  • 1st Person (I/Me): Intimate and personal. The listener feels like the singer.
  • 2nd Person (You): Direct. Either an attack or a love letter.
  • 3rd Person (He/She/They): Storytelling. Like watching a movie. Great for building a character's world.

6. Object Writing Exercise

Pick a physical object (a "cracked mirror" or "worn-out boots"). Write 5 sentences describing it. Now, use those descriptions to explain a human emotion. A worn-out boot isn't just leather; it's a person who has walked too far alone.
