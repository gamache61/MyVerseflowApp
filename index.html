Letter Theory Cheat Sheet

A: The "Anchor." The base rhyme sound.

B: The "Balance." The secondary sound.

ABAB: Cross Rhyme. Creates a walking, melodic feel.

AABB: Couplets. Punchy and high-energy.

ABCB: Storyteller mode. Only lines 2 and 4 rhyme for a natural feel.

The Songwriting Bible

Close

1. The 3-Act Structure

Think of your song like a movie:

  • Act 1 (Verse 1): Establish the "Normal." Where are we? Who is involved?
  • Act 2 (Verse 2): The "Complication." What changed? Why is there conflict?
  • Act 3 (Bridge): The "Resolution/Perspective." The moment of realization.

2. Melodic Math (1-2-3-4 Rule)

Consistency creates satisfaction. If line 1 is 8 syllables, line 2 should be 8 (symmetry) or 4 (half-time) to keep the listener's brain hooked.

3. Sensory Imagery (VAK)

Visual: Colors, lighting, objects.
Auditory: Echoes, whispers, specific sounds.
Kinesthetic: Textures, temperature, weight.

4. Stable vs. Unstable Rhymes

Perfect Rhymes (Cat/Hat): High closure. Good for the end of a chorus.
Slant Rhymes (Cat/Back): Low closure. Great for verses to keep the energy moving.

5. Object Writing Exercise

Pick a physical object and write only about its senses for 5 minutes. Use these as metaphors.
